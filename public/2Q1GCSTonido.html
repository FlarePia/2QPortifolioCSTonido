The Leni Robredo that we know today is a woman who fights for her beliefs and what is best for the country. Let's take a look at the journey of Leni Robredo

Leni's Life before Politics

Event Date Description
The Beginning April 23, 1965 Leni was born on April 23, 1965 in Naga, Camarines Sur.
Helping People in Need Leni's childhood Growing up, Leni's dad would bring home people that were in need. Her family would give them food, clothes, and allow them to take a bath in their home. Her father often asked her and her brother to give away their toys because many kids didn't have any toys at all. Leni was always raised in a home that promoted care, love and support.
Within Typhoons Leni's Childhood When there was a typhoon, people would come to her father and stay at their house for a while. Although their house wasn't big, they still warmly accomodated everyone and treated them with kindness. Leni would look forward to playing with the other kids whenever they came over to her house.
The Love Story August of 1986 Leni and Jesse met right after the EDSA People Power Revolution. He started courting her by September that year, asking for her hand in marriage by December. Although her parents initially opposed it, they were insistent and eventually got married on June 27, 1987. Leni said that she had never been more sure about marrying Jesse because there was just something so different about him.
Marriage Life June 27, 1987 - onward When Jesse ran for mayor of Naga City, Leni was initially very much against it. Over time however, Jesse was able to convince her otherwise. But they were very conscious about raising their kids to be as normal as possible. They were luckily able to maintain a very simple life.
The Death of a Loved One August 18, 2012 But on an unexpected day,Leni's husband, Jesse Robredo died in a plane crash in 2012. Leaving her the only parent of their 3 daughters: Tricia, Jillian and Aika. Only after her husband's death did she start getting involved in politics, a story that continues to be told until this day
Moving forward...she has inspired the hearts of many Filipinos and still continues to do so until this day.
